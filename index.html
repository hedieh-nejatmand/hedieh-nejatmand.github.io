Hedieh Nejatmand

PhD Student in Psychology · University of New Hampshire

I am a PhD student in cognitive psychology at the University of New Hampshire. My research examines how working memory, attention, and metacognitive processes influence learning and decision-making.

My current work focuses on the role of working memory in category learning, including how different cognitive demands affect rule-based and information-integration learning.

I am also interested in applying cognitive science to human factors, human–AI interaction, and the design of more intuitive and human-centered technologies.

Research interests: working memory · category learning · attention · metacognition · human factors · human–AI interaction

Research

My research investigates the cognitive processes that support learning and performance, with particular emphasis on individual differences in working memory and attention.

Selected Projects

Working Memory and Category Learning

Examining how verbal working-memory demands influence learning across rule-based and information-integration category structures. The project uses controlled behavioral experiments and trial-level performance analyses.

Metacognition and Memory

Investigating how prior beliefs and moment-to-moment experiences contribute to predictions and evaluations of memory performance.

Teaching

My teaching interests include introductory psychology, cognitive psychology, research methods, and helping students connect psychological theory with real-world examples.
